🎯 About This Workflow

This document defines the official submission workflow for the two available project tracks. Each track has its own focus area, eligibility criteria, and submission requirements. A project may only be submitted to one track — cross-track submissions are not permitted. Choose the track that best fits your project and follow its rules in full.

AI Track — Submission Rules

For AI, ML, deep learning, NLP, and computer vision projects · Teams of 1–5 · Deadline Jun 20, 2026

Required Deliverables
💻
Working Prototype / Demo
Link or executable · Max 500 MB if uploaded directly
📄
Technical Report (PDF)
10–25 pages · Problem, architecture, data, evaluation, results
💾
Source Code Repository
GitHub/GitLab link with clear README
🗄️
Dataset Declaration
Provenance, licensing, and usage description for all data
⚖️
AI Ethics Statement (1–2 pages)
Fairness, bias, privacy, and potential misuse addressed
👥
Team Bios & Roles
Max 1 page total · Name, role, and brief background
Submission Rules
IDRuleDetails
R1
Single Track Only
A project submitted to the AI Track may not be submitted to the Innovation Track.
R2
File Formats
Reports must be PDF. Code must be in a public/shared repository. Executables must run on Linux or Windows without special hardware unless declared.
R3
Model Transparency
All model architectures must be described in the technical report. Black-box submissions without any architectural disclosure will be rejected.
R4
Data Licensing
All training and evaluation data must be legally licensed for the intended use. Proprietary datasets must be declared.
R5
Reproducibility
The submission must include sufficient information (code, config, instructions) for reviewers to reproduce key results, even approximately.
R6
Ethics Compliance
Projects that demonstrably violate ethical AI principles (e.g., discriminatory models) will be disqualified regardless of technical quality.
R7
No Late Submissions
Submissions after June 20, 2026 at 23:59 UTC will not be accepted under any circumstances.
R8
Original Work
The AI component must be developed by the submitting team. Using third-party libraries is permitted; submitting others' models as your own is not.
R9
Demo Availability
Teams shortlisted for the final round must be available for a live demo/Q&A in the week of July 7–10, 2026.
R10
Language
All written deliverables must be submitted in English.
Evaluation Criteria
30%
Technical Innovation
Novelty of the AI approach beyond standard applications
25%
Technical Soundness
Correct implementation and statistically valid results
20%
Real-World Impact
Clarity and significance of the problem addressed
15%
AI Ethics
Depth of ethics statement; risks considered and mitigated
10%
Presentation
Quality of report, code docs, and demo clarity

Innovation Track — Submission Rules

For novel products, processes, and social impact without a primary AI/ML core · Teams of 1–8 · Deadline Jun 30, 2026

Required Deliverables
🔧
Prototype / Proof-of-Concept
Physical, digital, or video (≤10 min MP4/MOV)
📄
Impact Report (PDF)
8–20 pages · Problem, solution, validation, scalability
📈
Business / Deployment Model
How the solution reaches its users or beneficiaries
🔬
Market / Field Research Summary
Evidence that the problem is real and well-understood
⚠️
Risk Assessment (1 page)
Key risks and how they will be managed
👥
Team Bios & Roles
Max 1 page total · Name, role, and brief background
Submission Rules
IDRuleDetails
R1
Single Track Only
A project submitted to the Innovation Track may not be submitted to the AI Track.
R2
File Formats
Reports must be PDF. Videos must be MP4 or MOV, max 10 minutes — upload directly or share via YouTube/Vimeo (unlisted accepted).
R3
Evidence of Novelty
The impact report must include a section explaining what makes the approach novel compared to existing solutions. Generic claims without evidence will reduce scores.
R4
Prototype Requirement
A working prototype (not just slides or a concept doc) is mandatory. A video walkthrough is acceptable if a physical prototype cannot be shipped.
R5
Scalability Statement
Submissions must address how the solution scales beyond the prototype — commercially, operationally, or in terms of social reach.
R6
Ethical Considerations
Projects must briefly address any ethical, legal, or social implications of the proposed solution.
R7
No Late Submissions
Submissions after June 30, 2026 at 23:59 UTC will not be accepted under any circumstances.
R8
Original Work
The project must be substantially the work of the submitting team. Open-source foundations must be declared, and the team's original contribution must be clearly identified.
R9
Demo Availability
Shortlisted teams must be available for a live presentation/Q&A in the week of July 14–20, 2026.
R10
Language
All written deliverables must be submitted in English.
Evaluation Criteria
30%
Originality
How distinct is the solution compared to existing approaches?
25%
Feasibility
Is the prototype convincing and deployable in reality?
20%
Impact Potential
Breadth and depth of the problem addressed
15%
Scalability
Credibility of the plan to grow or replicate the solution
10%
Presentation
Quality of report, prototype demo, and communication
